Original Poetry

临江仙・偕友游云龙湖
雪霁云龙开净鉴，青山素裹参差。琼台深砌碎花瓷。风高松坠玉，日冷塔凝脂。 久立桥头呵冻手，笑谈鸥鹭迟迟。芦荻心事有谁知？扶摇身后远，多少拣寒枝。
Linjiangxian · Strolling with Friends by Yunlong Lake
The lake gleams like a mirror, snow just cleared away; Hills clad in pristine white stretch in a jagged array. The terraces seem paved with porcelain flecks so bright. Wind rocks the pines, snow falls like jade in sway; Sun chills the temple, there lies cream congealed in light. Long on the bridge we stand, warming our frozen hands, Chatting and laughing at gulls’ sluggish flight. Who knows the reeds’ thoughts along the strands? Behind me soar the dreams once grand— How many wintry branches I’ve picked in life’s lands.
绿萝
春秋同气宇，天地倚亭亭。满眼盘经纬，随心探止行。 堪居素罐浅，讵畏燕云冥。敷阴才不济，吐纳一室清。
Green Pothos
Through spring and fall, one constant grace, Between the sky and earth, upright in place. A full-eyed weave of warp and weft on show, It probes at will exactly where to go. Content to live in a shallow plain pot, What fear of dark northern clouds, however fraught? Its canopy is sparse, its shadow bare, Yet breathing, it purifies the chamber's air.
